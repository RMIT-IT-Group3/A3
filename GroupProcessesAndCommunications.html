Group Processes & Communications

Group communication method

Microsoft Teams has proven to be an extremely effective and productive communication tool for FlickDev Group and will remain the primary method for all group discussions and meetings. The Microsoft Teams video conference functions will be used and recorded for team meetings.

Members of the FlickDev Group will share their contact email addresses, for communication outside Microsoft Teams or invitations to collaborative tools, and personal mobile phone numbers for emergency reasons, such as when a member canâ€™t be contacted or is not responding to communications.


Meeting frequency

Per our established schedule, it is recommended that the group hold at least 2 video meetings per week using Microsoft Teams to discuss the project and address any challenges, as well as using the chat function to regularly post updates or related content. The tasks and scheduling functions of Microsoft Teams will allow members to track progress or due dates of task and allow for better project management.


Addressing a non-responsive group member

If a member is not responding to communications, or is not attending meetings the first step will be to try alternate contact methods such as email or phone. If no response has been received 5 days after this step the team manager will inform the RMIT course coordinator of the non-responsive group member.
