In examining and comparing our five ideal jobs, a common element we have identified is the importance of communication skills which was listed as a top general baseline skill for each position. To a slightly lesser extent, proficient writing skills, problem-solving abilities, and teamwork and collaboration skills also appeared as common elements.

When comparing IT-specific skills, we identified knowledge of Microsoft Windows, SQL, and programming and scripting languages like Java, JavaScript and PHP as common elements.

The most common jobs are the Cyber Security Specialist and Penetration Tester positions, which together form a specialised team that identifies possible security risks in an organisationâ€™s software or online services and develop solutions to address these vulnerabilities; although they may use different tools and technologies, they share a focus or providing online safety and security.

What differentiates each position

The jobs we have selected cover a broad range of the IT industry, from end-user support to internal team management. As a result, the main differences between the positions lie in the top IT-specific skills we have identified for each job: as an example, detailed knowledge of programming language HTML, used in a Web Developer role, will likely not be useful for a Technical Support representative.

There is also a significant difference in the day-to-day activities of each position and the different parties or teams they would interact with. While a Technical Support position might provide end-users with technical support and hardware installation, a Penetration Tester or Cyber Security Specialist may be monitoring networking traffic or testing applications for security vulnerabilities; while a Web Developer could be designing and developing a website or application, an ICT Manager can be resolving team conflicts or presenting new ideas to an organisation.

Career plans similarities and differences across the group

As the FlickDev Group members have all elected to study Information Technology online at RMT through Open Universities our career plans are largely similar at this point in our student careers. We all have a strong focus on completing our bachelor of IT with various minors specific to our desired career path. Some of us are planning to augment our study at RMIT with specific training through organisation such as Codeacademy or the Interaction Design Foundation and certifications such as AWS. Members are planning to either seek entry level experience within the industry or to make a sideways career move into a suitable position.
